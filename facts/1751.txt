The first volume of the Encyclopédie was published in France, attempting to contain all human knowledge in book form. The 18th century version of Wikipedia, but with better citations.
The Georgian era is in full swing in Britain, featuring elaborate wigs, questionable dental hygiene, and an impressive commitment to powdering everything.
Colonial America is experiencing growing tensions with Britain over taxation, though nobody's thrown any tea in harbors yet. Give it a couple decades.

Coffee houses are the social media of the era—people gather to share news, gossip, and hot takes. Some things never change.

Mail delivery takes weeks or months depending on distance. "Sorry for the late reply" actually means something.

Voltaire is 56 years old and has been annoying authority figures for decades. Satire as a weapon is alive and well.

Hot chocolate is a fashionable drink among the wealthy. Coffee and tea are also gaining popularity. Caffeine addiction spans centuries.

Absolute monarchy or constitutional limits? John Locke's ideas are spreading. Kings are not amused.

What makes someone a criminal—nature or society? Some argue poverty creates crime. Others insist criminals are born bad. Early criminology debates.
