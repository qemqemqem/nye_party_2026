The Georgian era is in full swing in Britain, featuring elaborate wigs, questionable dental hygiene, and an impressive commitment to powdering everything.
Colonial America is experiencing growing tensions with Britain over taxation, though nobody's thrown any tea in harbors yet. Give it a couple decades.

The novel as a literary form is just getting started. If you want entertainment, you're going to a play or reading very long poems.
France is building up debt under Louis XV that will eventually contribute to... well, let's just say 1789 gets interesting.
